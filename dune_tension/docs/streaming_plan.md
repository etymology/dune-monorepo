# Transition to streaking audio


## challenges
Major challenges of tension collection are 
 - getting the tensiometer actually focused on the wire, 
 - getting a pitched sound and distinguishing it from noise.
 - separate the sounds of wires from different layers: this problem occurs because the current layer being measured is only 5mm separated from the previous layer, which is on the order of the amount that the focus might need to be adjusted for a single plane. So it can be difficult to resolve focusing on a single plane.
 - resolving which wire we're acutally hearing when all we know about is relative positioning between wires, which can result in a drift of indices and getting off by a wire or two.

## current ways to address these challenges

focusing
 - we use a random wiggle of the focusing servo which moves the laser in the Z axis. Until a recent update this had the problem that when the servo motor moves the laser potiion in x also changed. This coupling should have been addressed but it's still not ideal to use a random wiggle. There's a correct depth at which to focus, and the "goodness" of the signal should be peak there, where the goodness is something like signal noise ratio where the signal is the pitched harmonic sound of the wire. This suggests that some kind of optimization problem is being solved by the autofocus which could be done more efficiently than a random wiggle
 - positionwise we also use a random wiggle around the predicted position for the measurement of the wire to find where the laser actually touches the wire. This works but is inefficient and because it's random it can accidentally measure skip a wire or measure a wire twice. The "goodness" or snr should also be continuous with position where the peak is along the length of the wire, which runs diagonally or roughly in the x direction. We know pretty well the wire spacing and the angle but neither of these is precisely known, though we could give the tension measuring system more calibrated data about the positions and angles of the wires which could be used instead of the incremental approach of now
 - The positionwise wiggling of the wire along the length of the wire either diagonally or in the x axis serves a second purpose: to be able to separate adjacent layers from each other. Adjacent layers are always at some angle to each other, and we are most likely to confuse adjacent layers, so by moving at the angle of the current layer we can try to get multiple measurements of one wire and avoid measuring a wire beneath it.

## collection issues
 - it can be difficult to distinguish between pitched and noise. Currently it's audible to the human ear but the tensiometer doesn't have a good way of classifying the goodness of the signal, so it's hard to do an optimization problem on focus or position without knowing what to maximize. Some attempts were made in spectrum analysis to gauge harmonicity as a proxy for snr because the signal is known to be harmonic. But this is not used in the final range. Amplitude is also not used at all, and it could be
 - we dont' currently have a way of continuously exciting a wire. The current method of pulsing air causes the wire to vibrate for a varying amount of time. Sometimes on the order of 5 seconds sometimes less than one. ideally we should be able to extract the pitch from even a brief sound but this is tricky. The air pulse also has an inherent refractory period after triggering because it uses a solenoid. This limits the frequency with which we can stimulate the wire. Ideally we would not stimulate an already vibrating wire because this can disrupt a good signal.

## streaming
The idea of sampling randomly and doing processing in between comes from the episodic nature of the wire stimulus with an air pulse. But the optimization space is continuous. So it would make more sense to be slowly moving to sweep over an area to find the best point and focus which should be independent dimensions. But this would require that we turn the audio in a a stream of audio/position artifacts. The area of correct focus is on the order of a hundred servo units and the positional focus on the order of 0.5mm and the command tolerance is 0.1mm. So we needn't collect positional data at a level much more precise than 0.5mm or focus data in this way, it is in principle a continuous space and we want to be optimizing over this space. Tracking the absolute position would also be useful for separating one wire in a layer from the next, and one layer from the other. 

one idea of measurement would be to move continuously through xy space while tracking audio and position, classifying that audio quickly in voiced and unvoiced audio, then classifying those by pitch using the neural network pesto which is a bit too slow for realtime classification. This would create a three dimensional pitch map of the apa with x y and focus, and the audio could then be analyzed in bulk afterwards rather than mixing data collection and analysis as currently. This is dependent on the possibility of continuously exciting the wire possibly by a continuous stream of air. This would require that we change radically data is collected stored and analyzed. What remains would be primarily the hardward interface and some of the pitch classification work but the rest would be totally restructured. This could perhaps be better conceived as a separate project rather than an upgrade of the current.
